Email notifications keep users informed when their favorite meals are served, so they never miss a dish they love.

## How it works

The notification system operates automatically:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add favorites">
    Save meals to your favorites list (at least one)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enable notifications">
    Turn on the email notification toggle in your Favorites page
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="System checks daily">
    When menus are updated, the system checks if any favorites appear
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Receive email">
    You get an email notification for each matching favorite meal
  </Step>
</Steps>

Notifications are sent to the email address associated with your Google account.

## Enabling notifications

To opt in to email alerts:

1. Navigate to **Favorilerim** (Favorites page)
2. Ensure you have at least one favorite meal
3. Locate the "E-posta Bildirimi" card at the top
4. Toggle the switch to the **on** position
5. Wait for the success toast: "E-posta bildirimleri açıldı"

The notification preference is saved immediately and syncs to the server.

<Note>
The email notification card only appears when you have at least one favorite meal saved.
</Note>

## Disabling notifications

To stop receiving email alerts:

1. Go to your Favorites page
2. Toggle the "E-posta Bildirimi" switch to **off**
3. See the confirmation: "E-posta bildirimleri kapatıldı"

You can re-enable notifications at any time without losing your favorites list.

## What triggers notifications

Emails are sent when:

- A new daily menu is published
- The menu contains at least one meal from your favorites
- You have notifications enabled
- The menu is for a current or future date

Notifications are **not** sent for:

- Past menus (already served)
- Meals you've removed from favorites
- Days when the cafeteria is closed
- Users who have disabled notifications

## Notification preferences storage

Your email notification preference is stored in the `emailPreferences` database table:

- **User-specific**: Each user has their own preference
- **Persistent**: Survives sign-out and sign-in
- **Synced**: Available across all devices
- **Timestamped**: Records when you last changed the setting

### Database structure

The system tracks:

- `userId`: Your unique user identifier
- `notifyFavorites`: Boolean (true = notifications on)
- `createdAt`: When you first set a preference
- `updatedAt`: Last time you changed the setting

## Backend API endpoints

The notification preference is managed via REST API:

**GET** `/api/email-preferences`
- Retrieves your current notification setting
- Returns: `{ notifyFavorites: true/false }`

**POST** `/api/email-preferences`
- Updates your notification preference
- Body: `{ notifyFavorites: true/false }`
- Response: Confirms the new setting

## Error handling

If toggling notifications fails:

- The switch reverts to the previous state
- An error toast appears: "Bir hata oluştu"
- You can try again immediately

The app uses optimistic updates:
1. UI toggles instantly
2. Request sent to server in background
3. If server rejects, UI reverts

## Privacy considerations

Email notifications respect user privacy:

- **Opt-in only**: Notifications are disabled by default
- **Clear disclosure**: The card shows exactly which email will receive alerts
- **Easy opt-out**: One-click toggle to disable
- **No spam**: Only sends when favorites actually appear
- **No sharing**: Your email and preferences are never shared with third parties

## Feature flag behavior

When authentication is disabled via feature flag:

- Email preferences return `notifyFavorites: false`
- The toggle is non-functional
- API endpoints return 503 (Service Unavailable)
- Users must wait for authentication to be re-enabled

This ensures no emails are sent when the auth system is in maintenance mode.

## Use cases

Common scenarios for email notifications:

1. **Busy schedules**: Get alerts even when you forget to check the app
2. **Popular meals**: Be notified early to arrive before the cafeteria runs out
3. **Dietary needs**: Receive alerts for vegetarian or allergen-friendly options
4. **Multiple favorites**: Track several meals without manual checking
5. **Planning**: Know in advance what days to eat at the cafeteria vs. elsewhere

Email notifications bridge the gap between passive browsing and active meal planning.
