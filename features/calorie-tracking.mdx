The calorie tracking feature allows users to log meals they've eaten and monitor their daily calorie consumption with visual charts and goal tracking.

## Logging consumed meals

Mark meals as consumed directly from the menu:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the meal">
    Locate the meal you ate on the daily menu
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click the plus icon">
    Click the circle-plus icon on the right side of the meal row
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Set calorie goal (first time)">
    If this is your first tracked meal, you'll be prompted to set a daily calorie goal
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm addition">
    A toast confirms: "[Meal name] ([calories] kcal) günlüğünüze eklendi"
  </Step>
</Steps>

The plus icon transforms into a checkmark to indicate the meal is logged.

<Warning>
You can only log meals for today or past dates. Future dates are blocked with an error: "Henüz günü gelmemiş bir yemeği ekleyemezsiniz."
</Warning>

## Removing logged meals

To remove a meal from your log:

**From menu**: Click the check icon (filled circle) next to a logged meal. It reverts to a plus icon with the toast "[Meal name] günlükten çıkarıldı."

**From calorie tracking page**: Click the trash icon next to the meal in the consumed meals list.

## Calorie tracking dashboard

Access the full tracking interface through:

- User dropdown → "Kalori Takibi"
- Direct navigation to `/kalori-takibi`

### Dashboard layout

The dashboard consists of:

1. **Header**: Back button, page title, and goal setting button
2. **Mini donut cards**: Horizontal scrollable timeline of logged days
3. **Navigation arrows**: Desktop overlays, mobile bottom buttons
4. **Big donut chart**: Detailed view of selected day
5. **Consumed meals list**: All meals logged for the selected day

## Setting calorie goals

### Initial goal setup

When you log your first meal:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Modal appears">
    A dialog prompts: "Günlük kalori hedefinizi belirleyin"
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter target calories">
    Input your daily calorie target (e.g., 2000)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save goal">
    Click "Kaydet" to save your goal
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Meal is logged">
    The meal you were adding is automatically logged after setting your goal
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Updating your goal

To change your calorie goal later:

1. Go to the calorie tracking page (`/kalori-takibi`)
2. Click the "Hedef: [X] kcal" button in the header
3. Enter your new target and save

The goal persists across all days and syncs to all your devices.

## Visual tracking: Donut charts

### Mini donut cards

The top of the dashboard shows 4-5 small donut charts in a horizontal row:

**Mobile**: 4 cards visible at once
**Desktop**: 5 cards visible at once

#### Card anatomy

- **Donut chart**: Visual representation of calories consumed vs. goal
- **Center number**: Total calories for that day
- **Date label**: "Bugün" (if today) or short date (e.g., "15 Mar")
- **Calorie fraction**: Desktop shows "[consumed]/[goal] kcal"

#### Chart colors

Each meal segment uses a distinct color from the palette:

```
Blue:     hsl(210, 70%, 65%)
Pink:     hsl(340, 65%, 65%)
Mint:     hsl(160, 55%, 55%)
Amber:    hsl(40, 80%, 60%)
Lavender: hsl(270, 55%, 68%)
Coral:    hsl(15, 75%, 65%)
Teal:     hsl(185, 60%, 55%)
Yellow:   hsl(55, 70%, 58%)
```

Remaining goal space is shown in light gray: `hsl(0, 0%, 90%)`

#### Selection state

Clicking a mini card:
- Adds a primary border and ring
- Enlarges slightly with shadow
- Updates the big chart below

### Big donut chart

The selected day's detailed view shows:

- **Large donut**: Bigger visualization with tooltips on hover
- **Center display**: 
  - Total calories (large, bold)
  - Goal fraction: "/ [goal] kcal" (smaller, muted)
- **Footer**:
  - Date label (e.g., "Pazartesi, 15 Mart 2025")
  - "Bugün" badge if it's today
  - Status badge (see below)

### Goal status indicators

The status badge shows your progress toward your goal:

**Green** (under 75% of goal):
- Badge text: "Hedefin altında"
- Color: `bg-emerald-500/10 text-emerald-600`

**Orange** (75-100% of goal):
- Badge text: "Hedefe yaklaşıyorsunuz"
- Color: `bg-amber-500/10 text-amber-600`

**Red** (exceeds goal):
- Badge text: "Hedef aşıldı"
- Color: `bg-red-500/10 text-red-600`

<Tip>
The status badge updates in real-time as you add or remove meals from your daily log.
</Tip>

## Consumed meals list

The right side (desktop) or below the chart (mobile) shows:

- **Section title**: "Yenilen Yemekler"
- **Meal rows**: Each with:
  - Color dot matching the donut chart segment
  - Meal name (title-cased)
  - Calorie badge (monospace font)
  - Eye icon: View meal details
  - Trash icon: Remove from log
- **Footer**: Total calories for the day with status-colored badge

### Empty state

```
Bu gün için kayıt yok.
```

This appears when a day is selected but no meals have been logged yet.

## Pagination and navigation

Browse through your calorie history:

**Desktop**: Hover arrows appear on the left and right sides of the mini donut cards. Click to navigate to previous or next page.

**Mobile**: "Önceki" and "Sonraki" buttons appear below the mini cards.

Each page shows 4-5 days depending on screen size. The app automatically starts on the most recent page when loading.

## Empty state (no logs)

When you haven't tracked any meals yet:

```
[Flame icon]

Kalori takibi için henüz bir kayıt bulunamadı. 
Menüdeki ekle butonunu kullanarak kalori 
takibine başlayabilirsiniz.

[Menüye Dön]
```

Click "Menüye Dön" to return to the menu and start logging meals.

## Data persistence

Your calorie logs are:

- **Stored per date**: Each day has its own independent log
- **Synchronized**: Available across all your signed-in devices
- **Permanent**: Logs remain in your account indefinitely
- **Optimistic**: UI updates instantly, syncs in background

### Automatic cleanup

When you remove all meals from a day, that day's record is deleted from the database entirely to keep your data clean.

## Use cases

Common ways students use calorie tracking:

1. **Diet monitoring**: Track intake to maintain or lose weight
2. **Meal planning**: Review past consumption to plan future meals
3. **Health goals**: Ensure adequate nutrition from cafeteria options
4. **Habit formation**: Build awareness of eating patterns
5. **Athletic training**: Monitor calories for workout performance

The visual charts make it easy to see trends and stay motivated toward your health goals.
